SAMPLE 3 — LOTTERY / PRIZE-WIN SCAM
Classification: PHISHING (High Risk)

--- Simulated Header ---
Return-Path: <claims@ms-lottery-intl.com>
Received: from mail.ms-lottery-intl.com [185.220.101.7]
SPF: FAIL
DKIM: none
Subject: Congratulations! You've Won $850,000 in the Microsoft Global Lottery

--- Email Body ---
Dear Winner,

Your email address was randomly selected as one of the lucky winners
of $850,000 USD in the Microsoft International Email Lottery Program.

To claim your prize, reply with your full name, home address, phone
number and a copy of your government-issued ID within 5 working days,
or your prize will be forfeited.

Mr. James Carter
Claims Department

--- Indicators Identified ---
1. Unsolicited, too-good-to-be-true windfall never entered into.
2. Direct request for government ID and personal address (identity theft).
3. Sender domain has no genuine connection to the company impersonated.
4. Artificial deadline ("5 working days") to pressure quick action.
5. Legitimate companies do not run lotteries that randomly select email addresses.
